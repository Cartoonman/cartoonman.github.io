DCS SELCAL

DCS stands for Digital Coded Squelch (also known as DCSS, Digitally Coded Squelch Signaling), and is used for selective calls and remote control applications.

The data is transmitted on sub audible tones within the frequency range 2 Hz to 300 Hz. In practice, DCS is similar to CTCSS, but uses 104 octal 3-digit code words instead of continuous analogue tones.

The data is sent as a series of 23-bit GOLAY encoded frames without any sync bits. Polarity can be normal or inverse, and must be selected by the user.
